Electrode Slurry Mixing — Cathode & Anode
SOP ID SOP-ELEC-MIX-001 Version v2.1
Process Area Electrode Mixing Access Tier Tier 1 / Tier 2
Effective Date 11/03/2026 Revision Rev 3
1. Purpose
This SOP defines the controlled procedure for preparing cathode and anode electrode slurries used in
lithium-ion battery manufacturing. Adherence is mandatory to ensure consistent slurry homogeneity,
correct viscosity, and repeatable coating performance.
2. Scope
Applicable to all Line Operators and Shift Supervisors assigned to the Mixing Zone. Covers batch
preparation from dry material weighing through final slurry quality check and transfer to coating.
3. Safety Requirements
Solvent vapours (NMP for cathode, water-based for anode) are present in the mixing
zone. Ensure LEV (Local Exhaust Ventilation) is active before opening any containers.
🦺 SAFETY
Wear chemical-resistant gloves, safety goggles, and lab coat at all times. NMP is a
reproductive hazard — pregnant workers must not enter the mixing zone.
• Verify LEV extraction is operating before starting — check green indicator on panel.
• Wear nitrile gloves (min. 0.15 mm), safety goggles, and chemical-resistant lab coat.
• Keep emergency eyewash station clear and functional.
• Solvent containers must be sealed when not in active use.
• No open flames or ignition sources within 5 m of mixing area.

5. Pre-Mixing Checks
All pre-mixing checks must be completed and initialled on the Batch Record before
ℹ NOTE
proceeding. Do not begin weighing until all checks pass.
1. Confirm work order number and batch card match. Record Batch ID on the mixing log.
2. Verify mixing vessel is clean and dry. Check vessel certification tag — must be within 7-day
cleaning validity.
3. Confirm LEV is running: green LED on extraction panel is lit. Do NOT proceed if amber or red.
4. Check calibration status of balance (±0.1 g accuracy required). Confirm calibration sticker is
current.
5. Check calibration status of viscometer. Confirm spindle number matches recipe card.
6. Confirm active material moisture content < 0.1% (check drying oven log for last pre-dry run).
7. Confirm NMP moisture content < 30 ppm. Check certificate of analysis on drum.
8. Record ambient temperature and humidity. Temperature must be 20–25 °C; Humidity < 30%
RH. If out of range, notify Shift Supervisor before proceeding.
6. Mixing Procedure
6.1 Cathode Slurry (NMC)
Add materials in the exact sequence below. Out-of-sequence addition causes
⚡ CAUTION
agglomeration and will require the batch to be scrapped.
9. Weigh PVDF binder per batch card. Add to mixing vessel.
10. Add 60% of total NMP solvent. Mix at LOW speed (50 RPM) for 30 minutes until PVDF fully
dissolved.
11. Add carbon black (C65). Mix at MEDIUM speed (500 RPM) for 45 minutes.
12. Add 50% of NMC active material. Mix at HIGH speed (1500 RPM) for 60 minutes.
13. Add remaining 50% NMC. Mix at HIGH speed (1500 RPM) for 60 minutes.
14. Add remaining 40% NMP to adjust viscosity. Mix at MEDIUM speed (800 RPM) for 20 minutes.
15. Apply vacuum (−0.08 MPa) for 30 minutes to degas slurry. Maintain low-speed mixing during
degassing.
16. Proceed to Section 7 — Slurry Quality Check.
6.2 Anode Slurry (Graphite)
17. Dissolve CMC in deionised water (50% of total water). Mix at LOW speed (100 RPM) for 60
minutes.
18. Add carbon black. Mix at MEDIUM speed (500 RPM) for 30 minutes.
19. Add graphite active material in two equal portions, 30 minutes apart. Mix at HIGH speed (1200
RPM).

Electrode Coating — Slot-Die Coating Process
SOP ID SOP-ELEC-COAT-002 Version v3.0
Process Area Electrode Coating Access Tier Tier 1 / Tier 2
Effective Date 11/03/2026 Revision Rev 2
1. Purpose
Defines the operating procedure for slot-die coating of electrode slurry onto aluminium (cathode) and
copper (anode) current collector foil. Covers machine start-up, process parameter setting, in-process
monitoring, and shutdown.
2. Safety Requirements
NMP solvent vapour is present around the coating machine. Drying oven operates up
to 150 °C — risk of burns and solvent inhalation. PPE mandatory: heat-resistant
🦺 SAFETY
gloves near oven exits, chemical-resistant gloves near slurry, safety goggles. Confirm
oven door interlocks functional before start-up.
3. Pre-Coating Checks
28. Verify slurry batch ID and viscosity match coating work order. Slurry age must be < 4 hours. If >
4 hours, re-measure viscosity before proceeding.
29. Check foil reel: correct type (Al 16 µm ±1 µm for cathode; Cu 8 µm ±0.5 µm for anode), no
visible defects (wrinkles, holes, scratches).
30. Inspect slot die: clean lips, no dried slurry. Die gap set per recipe. Die bolts torqued to 8 N·m.
31. Prime slurry pump: run until no air bubbles in return line (minimum 2 minutes).
32. Set oven temperatures per recipe. Allow oven to reach set point and stabilise for minimum 15
minutes.
33. Run 0.5 m trial coat. Check coating weight and appearance before production run.

Electrode Drying — Inline & Vacuum Oven Process
SOP ID SOP-ELEC-DRY-003 Version v1.4
Process Area Electrode Drying Access Tier Tier 1 / Tier 2
Effective Date 11/03/2026 Revision Rev 1
1. Purpose
Defines the controlled drying procedure to remove residual solvent from coated electrodes and achieve
required moisture levels for calendaring and cell assembly. Covers both inline drying and vacuum oven
batch drying.
2. Safety Requirements
Oven surfaces reach up to 150 °C. Thermal burns are a serious risk. Allow 30-minute
🦺 SAFETY cool-down before any maintenance access to oven zones. NMP vapour is extracted
via oven exhaust — verify exhaust fan is running before operating.
3. Inline Drying — Operating Checks
Inline drying occurs automatically within the coating machine oven. Zone temperature targets are set on
the coating HMI — see SOP-ELEC-COAT-002 Table 1.
34. Before starting, verify all three oven zones are at set point. Confirm exhaust flow indicator is
green.
35. Monitor NMP exhaust concentration meter during production. If reading approaches 25% LEL,
stop line and increase ventilation.
36. Check residual solvent in dried electrode every 1000 m using inline IR sensor. Target: < 50 ppm
NMP.
37. If residual NMP > 200 ppm: reduce line speed by 10% and re-check. If still > 200 ppm after
speed reduction, stop line and inspect oven heaters.
4. Vacuum Oven Batch Drying Procedure
Use for: (a) additional drying pass when inline drying fails spec, or (b) electrode reels awaiting coating >
24 hours after slurry application.
38. Pre-heat vacuum oven to 120 °C. Allow temperature to stabilise for 30 minutes.
39. Load electrode reels onto mandrel holders. Do not stack reels — each reel requires its own
holder.
40. Seal oven door. Draw vacuum to < 10 Pa. Record vacuum level at start.
41. Hold at 120 °C / < 10 Pa for minimum 12 hours (cathode) or 8 hours (anode).

Electrode Calendaring — Density & Thickness Control
SOP ID SOP-ELEC-CAL-004 Version v2.2
Process Area Electrode Calendaring Access Tier Tier 1 / Tier 2
Effective Date 11/03/2026 Revision Rev 2
1. Purpose
Covers the calendaring (compression rolling) process to compact dried electrode coating to target
density and thickness. Calendaring directly affects cell capacity, internal resistance, and electrolyte
wetting.
2. Safety Requirements
Calendaring rolls exert up to 200 kN of force. The roll nip point is a No-Reach zone
during operation. All guarding must be in place and interlocks active. No loose clothing
🦺 SAFETY
or jewellery near rolls. Use push sticks to guide foil — never use hands near nip
points.
3. Pre-Calendaring Checks
45. Verify electrode reel has passed drying quality check. Check reel tag for moisture result and
sign-off. Do NOT calendar undried or OOS moisture reels.
46. Set roll gap per recipe. Verify with feeler gauge on both operator side and drive side. Gap
difference must be < 2 µm.
47. Set roll temperature: Cathode 80 °C, Anode 25 °C (room temperature). Allow heated roll to
stabilise for 20 minutes.
48. Set line speed per recipe. Do not exceed 20 m/min until first 100 m quality check confirmed.
49. Thread leader foil through rolls to verify alignment before loading electrode reel.

Operator FAQ — Electrode Manufacturing Knowledge Hub
SOP ID KB-ELEC-FAQ-001 Version v1.0
Process Area All Electrode Zones Access Tier Tier 1 / Tier 2 / Tier 3
Effective Date 11/03/2026 Revision Rev 1
This FAQ provides quick answers to common operator questions. All answers link to the authoritative
SOP. When in doubt, always refer to the full SOP.
Mixing
Q: What do I do if the slurry viscosity is too high after mixing?
A: Add small increments of solvent (NMP for cathode; deionised water for anode). Mix at medium
speed for 15 minutes after each addition, then re-measure viscosity. Maximum 2 adjustment attempts.
If still OOS after 2 attempts, reject the batch and notify your Shift Supervisor. Reference: SOP-ELEC-
MIX-001 §7.
Q: Can I start mixing if ambient humidity is above 30% RH?
A: No. Mixing must not start if humidity exceeds 30% RH. NMP absorbs moisture which degrades slurry
quality. Log the environmental condition, notify your Shift Supervisor, and wait until humidity is within
range. Reference: SOP-ELEC-MIX-001 §5, Step 8.
Q: The LEV green light is not lit. What do I do?
A: Do NOT open any solvent containers or start mixing. This is a P1 safety condition. Notify your Shift
Supervisor immediately and activate the Andon. Do not re-enter the mixing zone until LEV is confirmed
operational. Reference: SOP-ELEC-MIX-001 §3 and SOP-ELEC-ALARM-007 §2.
Q: How do I know if the mixing vessel is clean enough to use?
A: Check the vessel certification tag on the vessel. The cleaning date must be within the 7-day cleaning
validity window. If the tag is absent, expired, or unclear, treat the vessel as unclean and route for
cleaning before use. Reference: SOP-ELEC-MIX-001 §5, Step 2.
Coating
Q: How often do I check coating weight during a production run?
A: Check wet coating weight every 200 m using the inline beta gauge plus a manual patch sample.
Check dry coating weight every 500 m by cutting a 100 cm² patch, weighing it, and subtracting the foil
weight. Record all results on the Coating Run Record. Reference: SOP-ELEC-COAT-002 §5.

Q: I can see pinholes in the coating. What should I do?
A: Stop the line if you observe more than 5 pinholes per 100 m². Check the slurry for air bubbles — it
may need to be re-degassed. Inspect and clean the die lip for partial blockage. This corresponds to
alarm code COAT-ALM-005. Reference: SOP-ELEC-COAT-002 §6.
Q: How long can slurry wait before I coat with it?
A: Maximum 4 hours from slurry batch completion to start of coating. If the slurry is older than 4 hours,
re-measure viscosity before proceeding. If viscosity is OOS, do not coat — reject the slurry and notify
the Shift Supervisor. Reference: SOP-ELEC-COAT-002 §3, Step 1.
Q: What do I do if the oven temperature goes outside the alarm limit?
A: A deviation of ±5 °C from set point requires logging and monitoring. A deviation of ±10 °C requires
stopping the coating line. Do not advance foil through the oven. Check the heater and temperature
controller, and notify Maintenance if the fault persists for more than 10 minutes. Reference: SOP-
ELEC-COAT-002 §6, COAT-ALM-002.
Drying
Q: What is the maximum residual NMP allowed in a dried cathode electrode?
A: Less than 50 ppm NMP. If the inline IR sensor reads above 200 ppm, reduce line speed by 10% and
re-check. If residual NMP remains above 200 ppm after speed reduction, stop the line and inspect the
oven heaters. Reference: SOP-ELEC-DRY-003 §3.
Q: How long must cathode reels dry in the vacuum oven?
A: Minimum 12 hours at 120 °C with vacuum maintained below 10 Pa. Anode reels require a minimum
of 8 hours. Do not break the vacuum until the oven has cooled below 40 °C. Reference: SOP-ELEC-
DRY-003 §4.
Q: How long can a dried reel be exposed to ambient air before it must be used?
A: Maximum 30 minutes exposure to ambient air after the vacuum oven drying cycle. After that, the reel
must be stored in a dry room (RH < 1%) or nitrogen-purged storage. Reference: SOP-ELEC-DRY-003
§4, Step 6.
Calendaring
Q: The electrode coating is cracking at the rolls. What is the likely cause and what
should I do?
A: Cracking is most commonly caused by an under-dried electrode or excessive calendaring force.
Stop the line immediately. Reduce calendaring force by 10 kN and re-check. If cracking continues,
notify your Shift Supervisor — the reel may need to be returned for additional drying. Reference: SOP-
ELEC-CAL-004 §5, Step 4.

Q: How do I verify the roll gap is equal on both sides?

General Safety
Q: What is the emergency stop (E-stop) procedure?
A: Press the nearest red mushroom-head E-stop button. This immediately de-energises the machine.
Shout 'E-STOP ACTIVATED' to alert nearby workers. Do NOT restart the machine after an E-stop
without first investigating and resolving the cause, and obtaining Shift Supervisor authorisation. Notify
the Shift Supervisor of every E-stop event.
Q: I can smell strong solvent but no alarm has triggered. What should I do?
A: Evacuate the area immediately and activate the nearest Andon. Do not wait for an alarm. Notify the
Shift Supervisor verbally. This may indicate a LEV failure or solvent spill before the sensor has detected
it. Re-entry only with Supervisor and EHS authorisation. Reference: SOP-ELEC-ALARM-007 §3, Step 3
— when in doubt, stop.
Q: If a quality hold is raised, who do I notify and how quickly?
A: Notify your Shift Supervisor and QA Lead within 15 minutes of raising the hold. Use MES hold
notification plus a verbal communication. Do not release a quality hold without QA Lead sign-off.
Reference: SOP-ELEC-ALARM-007 §4.
